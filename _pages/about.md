## About Me
Hi, I'm Leif Lui, a visiting/fellow scholar at the Beijing Institute of Mathematical Science and Applications (BIMSA). I am a theoretical physicist working on gravitational waves (GWs), black-hole perturbation theory (BHPT), self-force (SF) theory, and extreme-mass-ratio inspirals (EMRIs). As a core member of the astrophysics and waveform working groups (WGs), my work mainly consists of developing waveform models that incorporate environmental effects (i.e. aerodynamics, turbulence, resonance, shocks, scattering, etc.) in preparation for space-based GW detectors like the Laser Interferometer Space Antenna (LISA), TianQin (天琴), and Taiji (太极). Hopefully, my research can elucidate the nature of black holes and their astrophysical environments.
